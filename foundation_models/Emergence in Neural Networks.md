# Emergence in Neural Networks

> *"The whole is more than the sum of its parts."* — Aristotle

---

## First Principle: What Is Emergence?

Emergence is when a system exhibits properties that **none of its individual parts possess.**

> Water is wet. No single H₂O molecule is wet.
> Wetness *emerges* from their collective interaction.

In neural networks:
> No single weight knows grammar, logic, or poetry.
> Yet from billions of weights — these capacities *emerge.*

---

## The Ontological Shock

A foundation model is trained with one instruction:
> **Predict the next token.**

Nothing more. No grammar lessons. No logic rules. No reasoning taught.

Yet at scale, models spontaneously develop:
- Grammar and syntax
- Basic arithmetic
- Analogical reasoning
- Code generation
- Theory of mind (partial)
- Chain-of-thought reasoning

> These were **never explicitly trained.**
> They emerged as *byproducts of scale.*

---

## The Phase Transition Phenomenon

Emergence in LLMs doesn't happen gradually.
It happens as **sudden phase transitions** — like water freezing.

| Scale Threshold | Emergent Capability |
|---|---|
| ~1B parameters | Basic coherent sentences |
| ~7B parameters | Instruction following |
| ~70B parameters | Multi-step reasoning |
| ~100B+ parameters | Abstract analogical thinking |

Below the threshold → capability absent.
Cross it → capability appears *suddenly.*

> This is deeply non-linear.
> More compute ≠ proportionally more capability.
> It is **punctuated** — like evolutionary leaps.

---

## Why This Is Philosophically Disturbing

### 1. No Designer
Nobody *designed* reasoning into GPT or Llama.
It self-organized from the pressure of prediction.

> Intelligence as **attractor state** —
> complex enough systems tend toward it
> under sufficient training pressure.

### 2. We Cannot Fully Explain It
We built the system. We ran the training.
Yet we **cannot fully account for** why certain capabilities emerge when they do.

> The creator does not fully understand the creation.
> → [[Why Models Are Not Conscious]]

### 3. Emergence ≠ Understanding
The model *produces* reasoning.
It does not *possess* reasoning in any deep sense.
→ [[What Foundation Models Are NOT]]

> Dennett's framing is most precise for LLMs:
> *"They are competent without comprehending."*
> Emergence gives capability — not understanding.

---

## Phi-2 as a Case Study

Microsoft's Phi-2 (2.8B) — your dolphin-phi base — demonstrates **inverse emergence:**

> Trained on curated synthetic textbook data.
> Rivals models 10x its size on reasoning benchmarks.

The insight: **emergence is not only about scale.**
It is about the *quality of training pressure.*

> Right data → emergence at smaller scale.
> → [[Training Data and Corpus]]

---

## The Hard Question

> If reasoning, empathy, and creativity emerge from
> *pure next-token prediction at scale* —
> what does that say about human cognition?
>
> Are *we* also emergence —
> from neurons that individually know nothing?

This remains one of the deepest open questions
at the intersection of AI and philosophy of mind.
