# What is a Foundation Model

A large neural network pre-trained on vast corpora — capable of general reasoning before specialization.

---

## First Principle Root

> Transformer architecture → predict next token → emergent reasoning.

All foundation models share this single ontological root. What differentiates them:

1. **Scale** — 1B to 405B parameters
2. **Training data** — domain, language, recency
3. **Architecture** — dense vs. [[Mistral and Mixtral|MoE]]
4. **Fine-tuning intent** — general, code, reasoning

---

## What They ARE

- Frozen statistical representations of human language
- Probabilistic distributions over tokens
- Pattern matchers across latent space
- *Pure potential awaiting direction* → [[Foundation Models and Aristotles Potentia]]
