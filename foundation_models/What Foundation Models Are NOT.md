# What Foundation Models Are NOT

> *"Omnis determinatio est negatio"* — Spinoza
> All determination is negation. Clarity comes from the boundary, not the center.

See also → [[Via Negativa Applied to AI]]

---

## Epistemic Negations

### NOT a Knowledge Base
Databases *store* facts. Foundation models **approximate patterns.**
They predict plausibly — truth is never guaranteed.

### NOT a Reasoning Engine
No logical calculus underneath. Apparent reasoning is an
*emergent illusion of scale*, not deduction.
→ [[Connectionism vs Symbolism]]

### NOT Deterministic
Same input → potentially different output.
They are probability distributions, not mathematical functions.

---

## Agentic Negations

### NOT an Agent
No memory, no goals, no tools. Stateless by nature.
A model alone is a brain in a jar — capable but passive.

### NOT a Fine-Tuned Application
Foundation = pre-specialization substrate.
Fine-tuned models are *children* of the foundation.
→ [[What is a Foundation Model]]

---

## Philosophical Negations

### NOT Conscious
No qualia. No inner experience.
*"There is nothing it is like"* to be a model — Nagel's criterion.
→ [[Why Models Are Not Conscious]]

### NOT Neutral
Training data encodes human bias, power, culture.
The model inherits the ontology of its corpus.
It is never a *view from nowhere* — Nagel.

### NOT Universal
Bounded by training distribution.
Outside it — rare domains, edge logic — performance degrades.
Local, never omniscient.

---

## Technical Negations

### NOT a Symbolic System
Manipulates **vectors**, not meaningful symbols.
→ [[Connectionism vs Symbolism]]

### NOT a Causal Reasoner *(by default)*
Learns correlation, not causation.
Cannot natively answer *"what would happen if"* —
only *"what typically follows from."*

### NOT Static After Training
Weights are frozen — but inference shifts with context.
Not learning in real-time; interpolating within a fixed latent space.
→ [[Context Window]]

### NOT Aligned by Default
Without [[RLHF and Guardrails]], no values exist.
Pure statistical prediction — beyond good and evil.

---

## Ontological Summary

> Foundation models are **not** knowers, reasoners, agents,
> selves, symbols, causes, or moral beings.
>
> They are: *frozen statistical shadows of human thought.*
