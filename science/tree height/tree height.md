2. **What it is?**
	1. Height improves biomass accuracy:**
	- Many allometric equations use BOTH diameter AND height (Biomass = a × DBH^b × Height^c) to calculate biomass more precisely - using height can reduce estimation error from ±30% (DBH only) to ±15% (DBH + height), which matters when selling carbon credits worth thousands of dollars
	
	2. Height validates your measurements and detects problems:
	- The height-diameter relationship is predictable for healthy forests - if your trees have normal DBH but abnormally short height, it signals problems (poor soil, water stress, disease, competition) that will reduce your carbon projections, allowing early intervention before the whole project fails
