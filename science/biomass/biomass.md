**1. What it is:**

- biomass is defined as the oven dry weight of trees and other forest vegetation.
- Different parts of tree component has its own biomass percentage as shown below.
- The quantity of carbon can then be estimated by converting biomass to carbon using the IPCC default carbon fraction of 0.47. Below ground tree root biomass is commonly estimated as a ratio of the aboveground tree biomass (source: https://winrock.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/Winrock-AllometricEquationGuidance-2016.pdf).

**2. Why we need it for ARR:**
1. **Biomass IS the carbon you're selling:**
- Carbon credits are calculated from biomass (dry weight of trees) - specifically, biomass × 0.47 = carbon tons, and carbon × 3.67 = CO₂ equivalent tons that buyers pay for - so biomass is literally the "product".

2. **Biomass connects measurements to money:**
- It's the middle step in the value chain: DBH (what you measure) → allometric equations → **biomass** (biological reality) → carbon content → CO₂ credits (what you sell) - without understanding biomass, you can't translate "I have 1000 trees with 25cm diameter" into "I have 150 tons of carbon worth $15,000"
