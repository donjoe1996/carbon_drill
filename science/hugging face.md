**Insight:**  
**Hugging Face** is a company and open-source community that makes it easy to use powerful AI models, especially for language. It started in 2016 and became widely known after releasing its [[transformers]] library in 2018, which helped bring modern “attention-based” models into everyday use 🙂

**High-level:**  
In plain English, Hugging Face is like a big, **friendly library and workshop for AI**. You can go there to download ready-made models, try them in a few lines of code, or share your own. Researchers and companies use it to build things like chatbots, translators, summarizers, and text analyzers. The idea is to remove the heavy engineering work so people can focus on the problem they want to solve (Hugging Face company history, 2016; Transformers library releases, 2018–present).

**Detail:**  
The company began in 2016, but its major impact came when it released the open-source [[transformers]] library in 2018. That library packaged research ideas from the Transformer architecture (first published in 2017 in “Attention Is All You Need”) into tools that anyone could run. Since then, Hugging Face has also built a public “model hub” where thousands of pretrained models are shared and reused, and tools that standardize training and inference. These facts are documented in the original research paper (2017) and Hugging Face’s official library and platform releases (from 2018 onward).

**Core concept:**  
Hugging Face = a company and open platform (founded 2016) that makes advanced AI models easy to access and use, best known for its Transformers library (since 2018) and its shared model hub. In short, it helps turn cutting-edge AI research into practical tools people can use every day 🌱
