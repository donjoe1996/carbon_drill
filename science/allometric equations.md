**1. What it is:**
- keywords: "Scaling relationship" 
- A mathematical formula (typically Biomass = a × DBH^b) that predicts one tree dimension from another - like using diameter to calculate total weight, height, or carbon content without destructively measuring the tree
- One well known allometric equation for **height-diameter relationship** is known as [[Schumacher equation]] (Schumacher and Hall, 1933)


**2. Why we need it for ARR:**
- Converts the easy-to-measure DBH into the hard-to-measure biomass and carbon stocks, enabling non-destructive monitoring of thousands of trees - essentially it's the calculator that turns "35cm diameter" into "450 kg of carbon stored"
