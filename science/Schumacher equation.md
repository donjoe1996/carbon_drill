1. What is it?
	- Schumacher is a PERSON (Francis X. Schumacher, 1930s forestry researcher), not one specific equation.
	- He developed multiple equations for different forestry problems. So "Schumacher equation" is ambiguous - it depends on context.
2. What tree component relationship can be understood using this equation?
	**a. [[tree height]]-diameter equation** 
		"As trees get fatter (larger [[tree diameter]]), they get taller (H), but the height increase slows down as they grow."
		$$H = exp(b₀ + b₁/DBH)$$
	**b. volume-height-diameter**
		"As trees get thicker and taller, the [[volume]] increases RAPIDLY (following a power relationship), not quite exponentially."
		$$V = a × DBH^b × H^c$$
		
3. Why is it this equation is well known?
	1. **Well-tested** - used since 1930s, validated on millions of trees
	2. **Mathematically sound** - logarithmic form is statistically robust
	3. **Widely published** - thousands of papers use it, easy to compare
	4. **Flexible** - works across many species and forest types
